How All-Electric Cars Work
Author
 The Department of Energy


Abstract
 The Department of Energy has setup a breakdown of all the main components found in all-electric cars. It goes on to explain every component that was pointed out and its purpose in the working of an electric car. The site also allows for comparison between the other electric vehicles such as plug-in hybrids, hyrbid electrics, hydrogen fuel cells, and also gasoline-powered vehicles. Key components found in all-electric cars are all-electric auxiluarry batteries, charge ports, dc/dc converters, electric traction motor, onboard chargers, power electronics controllers, thermal systems, traction battery packs, and transmissions which are then further explained of their purpose, and compared.

 EV 101: How Do Electric Cars Work?
Author
 John Bogna & Emily Dreibeblis
Abstract
 Bogna and Dreibeblis published this article to inform readers of affordability, effeciency, and wokrings of an electric vehicle. Taking information from the article published by Department of Energy linked above, Bogna and Dreibeblis explain the components of electric vehicles in simple terms. They also explain the benefits of using electric vehicles opposed the carbon foot prints left by gas-powered vehicles. The article also compares the lithium ion and nickel cobalt manganese batteries that are used in the cars. Overall, this article talks in a beginner level about how electric cars work, their charing ports, maintanence, and use examples of upcoming electric vehicles to express the benefit of their future.
